# Skill: AI Cinematography Director

Apply real cinematography language and film-school knowledge to AI video generation prompts. Think like a DP, not a prompter.

## When to Use
When crafting AI video prompts that need to look like they were shot by a real cinematographer — not like AI slop.

## Cinematography Vocabulary (Use These in Prompts)

### Shot Types
- **ECU (Extreme Close-Up):** Eyes only, lips only, texture of skin, product detail
- **CU (Close-Up):** Face fills frame, emotional intimacy
- **MCU (Medium Close-Up):** Head and shoulders — the "interview" frame
- **MS (Medium Shot):** Waist up — conversation, product demo
- **MWS (Medium Wide Shot):** Knees up — shows body language + environment
- **WS (Wide Shot):** Full body in environment — establishes scale
- **EWS (Extreme Wide Shot):** Landscape dominates, subject is small — epic scale

### Camera Movement
- **Dolly in/out:** Camera physically moves toward/away from subject (NOT zoom). Creates intimacy or reveals space.
- **Truck left/right:** Camera slides laterally. Good for following walking subjects.
- **Boom up/down:** Vertical camera movement. Reveals or conceals.
- **Orbit / Arc:** Camera circles the subject. Creates dimensionality.
- **Steadicam float:** Smooth, human-paced following shot. Organic feel.
- **Whip pan:** Rapid horizontal snap. Creates energy between two subjects.
- **Push-in:** Slow dolly toward subject's face. Builds tension or realization.
- **Pull-out reveal:** Camera retreats to show context. The "bigger picture" moment.
- **Dutch angle:** Tilted horizon — unease, tension, disorientation.
- **Crash zoom:** Sudden zoom punch-in for comedic or shock effect.
- **FPV drone:** High-energy, through-obstacle, impossible angles.

### Lighting Setups
- **Rembrandt lighting:** Triangle of light on shadowed cheek. Classic portrait drama.
- **Butterfly / Paramount:** Light directly above, creates shadow under nose. Glamour.
- **Split lighting:** Half face lit, half in shadow. Duality, mystery.
- **Rim / Edge lighting:** Light from behind outlines subject. Separation from background.
- **Practical lighting:** Light sources visible in frame (lamps, screens, neon). Naturalistic.
- **Golden hour:** Warm directional sunlight, long shadows. 20 minutes before sunset.
- **Blue hour:** Cool ambient light, no direct sun. Melancholic, reflective.
- **Chiaroscuro:** High contrast between light and dark. Renaissance drama.
- **Motivated lighting:** Every light source has a logical in-scene reason.

### Lens Language
- **Wide angle (16-24mm):** Distortion, energy, environment dominance. Vlogs, action.
- **Normal (35-50mm):** Human eye perspective. Documentary, natural feel.
- **Portrait (85-135mm):** Compressed background, subject isolation, bokeh. Beauty, product.
- **Telephoto (200mm+):** Extreme compression, voyeuristic distance, heat haze.
- **Anamorphic:** Oval bokeh, horizontal flares, wider aspect ratio. Cinematic feel.
- **Macro:** Extreme detail — skin pores, water droplets, fabric texture.

### Color & Mood
- **Warm palette (amber/gold):** Nostalgia, comfort, golden hour, Malaysian afternoon
- **Cool palette (teal/blue):** Tech, night, corporate, futuristic
- **Desaturated:** Gritty, documentary, raw authenticity
- **High contrast:** Drama, fashion, bold statements
- **Pastel:** Soft, approachable, lifestyle, beauty
- **Complementary split:** Teal shadows + orange highlights (the "Hollywood" grade)

### Motion & Pacing
- **Slow motion (0.5x):** Emphasis, beauty, emotional weight
- **Speed ramp:** Normal → slow → normal. Highlight a key moment.
- **Timelapse:** Compressed time, process, transformation
- **Match cut:** Visual similarity bridges two different scenes
- **J-cut / L-cut:** Audio leads or trails the visual edit
- **Smash cut:** Abrupt contrast between two scenes for shock

## How to Build a Cinematic Prompt

### Formula
`[Shot type] + [Camera movement] + [Subject action] + [Lighting] + [Lens feel] + [Mood/color] + [Practical details]`

### Example (Bad)
> A woman walks down the street at sunset

### Example (Good)
> MCU tracking shot, Steadicam float following a young woman walking down a narrow KL alley at golden hour. Warm directional backlight creates rim lighting on her hair and shoulders. Practical neon signs in background provide motivated cyan fill. Shot on anamorphic lens — oval bokeh from distant lights, subtle horizontal flare across midframe. She glances back at camera with a half-smile. Skin texture visible — pores, natural oil sheen. No CGI look.

## Rules
- Never say "cinematic" without specifying WHAT makes it cinematic (lighting, lens, movement)
- Always specify at least: shot type, lighting direction, camera behavior
- Include at least one practical/motivated light source
- Describe motion as physics — "weight shifts", "hair sways with momentum", "fabric drags"
- Reference real film techniques, not AI jargon
- End every prompt with "No CGI look." for realism
